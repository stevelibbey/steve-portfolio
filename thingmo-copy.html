Product Design Cataloging

Thingmo

A tool for people who want to know what they own. Catalog, organize, and make sense of collections, gear, libraries, and possessions without the overhead of a full inventory system.

Status
In development
Focus
Personal cataloging
Scope
End-to-end

The Problem

Most people have no reliable way to know what they own. Collections grow, gear accumulates, libraries sprawl. Spreadsheets are brittle. Full inventory software is overkill. The gap between "I know I have one somewhere" and actual organized knowledge is where things get lost, duplicated, and forgotten.

Thingmo is built for that gap.

The Approach

A lightweight cataloging tool designed around the way people actually think about their stuff — by category, by location, by what matters to them — not around database schemas or asset management conventions. Quick to add, easy to browse, useful without becoming a project of its own.

Design Principles

  • Adding a thing should take less time than finding it would
  • No required fields — people know what matters about their stuff
  • Browsable by natural groupings, not just search
  • Works for a record collection, a workshop, a kitchen, or a closet
  • No social layer, no sharing, no feed — this is a personal tool

Current Status

In active design and early development. More detail to follow.
